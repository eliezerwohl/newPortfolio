My Story

Or How I Learned to Stop Worrying and Love the Code

    I’ve been working on websites for many years, since the days of Homestead, Xanga, and GeoCities. I’ve always had a desire to do more with this hobby. In 2015, I saw an ad for a Rutgers Coding Bootcamp, and I was immediately intrigued. I was impressed by the Javascript based full stack curriculum. Ever since I stepped into the first class, I’ve been hooked. I’ve been coding and improving my skills every day.


    Since finishing the course, my life has changed. I was able to leave my previous job and turn what was previously a hobby into a career. I've also enjoyed being able to create more complex and rewarding side projects.

Portfolio

matched connections

My dating website/missed connection improvement/reddit substitute/chat app. A user can sign up, and match other people together for internet points. They can also fill out their own profiles, to be matched by other people. Frontend:JavaScript/CSS3/jQuery. Backend: Socket.IO, Express/Handlebars.js

liberty vice

Website commisioned for local band. I was responsible for the full-stack development, with an emphasis on front end presentation and mobile responsiveness. Frontend:JavaScript/CSS3/jQuery. Backend: Express/Handlebars.js

Heroku Caffeine

A program I wrote for my phone that prevents my Heroku sites from going to "sleep". Can be set to run and keep the sites up for a period of time, or it can wake all the sites up at once for presentation purposes. Developed using Android Studio and Java.

king of dice

A companion app to the King Of New York board game. I also developed a version for android with motion controls and an option to roll normal dice.
